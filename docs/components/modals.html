Best practices

Modals should:

  • Be used for tasks which require an explicit action
  • Be used for focused, specific tasks
  • Don't include more than one primary call to action

Content guidelines

Title

Titles should be:

  • Informative: the title should label the modal's content.
  • Concise: say in one word what you might say in three.
  • Scannable: keep titles to a single sentence, avoid punctuation, and avoid articles (i.e. the, a, an) to keep content short.
  • Write in sentence case (first word capitalize, the rest is lowercase).

Content

Content should be:

  • Actionable: start sentences with imperative verbs to inform what actions are available. Don't use permissive language such as "you can".
  • Structured: Don't bury important information. Put it first. Users will read the first part and begin skimming if they can't locate what they need.
  • Clear: when something is required, be direct to help users understand what they need to do in order to accomplish their task.

Actions

Actions should be:

  • Clear: users should understand and anticipate what will happen when they engage with an action. Don't mislead or create confusion by mislabeling a button.
  • Actionable: Lead with a strong verb that describes and encourages the action. To provide enough context, usually you will need to use a [verb] + [noun] combination. In some cases, a strong verb label can be enough (e.g. "Save", "Cancel", "Close", etc).
  • Concise: keep labels scannable by avoiding unnecessary words and articles.

Behavior

Since Stacks’s scope is limited to CSS and HTML, the JS found on these pages is intended to communicate behavior only.

Modals should be presented with a semi-transparent blocker to focus the user’s attention on the task being presented. Practically, on Stack Overflow, most pages will have a div with the class .s-modal-overlay somewhere in the view. You’ll need to include one if it’s not. The modal experience is not complete without the overlay. In addition, our modals are not feature-complete unless the following behaviors are enabled:

  1. The user can close the modal dialog with their ESC key.
  2. The user can click outside the dialog to close it.
